There are two decompositions of test error you keep meeting in [[machine-learning-theory]]. They look different and arise in different communities, but they are answering the same question — *where does the error come from?* — at different granularities.

## Bias–variance (statistics)

For squared loss, expected test error at $x_0$ splits into
$$
R(x_0) = \text{bias}^2 + \text{variance} + \sigma^2.
$$
This is the lens in [[./bias-variance-refresher|the bias–variance refresher]]. It is local (per $x_0$), assumes squared loss, and is silent about *why* a particular learner has the bias and variance it does.

## Approximation / estimation / optimization (learning theory)

Fix a hypothesis class $\mathcal{H}$ and a loss $\ell$. Write $f^\star \in \arg\min_f \mathbb{E}\,\ell(f(X), Y)$ for the Bayes optimum, $h^\star \in \arg\min_{h \in \mathcal{H}} \mathbb{E}\,\ell(h(X), Y)$ for the best in class, and $\hat h$ for what the optimizer actually returns. Then
$$
\underbrace{\mathbb{E}\,\ell(\hat h, Y) - \mathbb{E}\,\ell(f^\star, Y)}_{\text{excess risk}}
= \underbrace{\mathbb{E}\,\ell(h^\star, Y) - \mathbb{E}\,\ell(f^\star, Y)}_{\text{approximation}}
+ \underbrace{\mathbb{E}\,\ell(\hat h, Y) - \mathbb{E}\,\ell(h^\star, Y)}_{\text{estimation + optimization}}.
$$
The estimation/optimization term is often split again: how close $\hat h$ is to the empirical minimizer (optimization), and how close the empirical minimizer is to $h^\star$ (estimation).

<Callout type="tip" title="Why two decompositions">
The bias–variance split asks *what does the cloud of trained models look like?* The approximation/estimation/optimization split asks *which of three pipeline stages is the bottleneck?* They are answers to different questions at different scales.
</Callout>

## When each is the right lens

- **Use bias–variance** when you control the model class and want to predict what *more capacity* or *more data* will do.
- **Use approximation/estimation/optimization** when you are diagnosing a system end-to-end and need to know whether to change the class, gather data, or tune the optimizer.
