This explainer treats chain-of-thought as an intermediate representation that can reveal useful information about a model's computation. It belongs to [[machine-learning-theory]] and appears in the [[research-papers]] hub.

## Setup

Let $X$ be an input, $Y$ be a target, and $Z$ be a chain-of-thought transcript sampled from a model.

<MathBlock title="Information carried by a rationale">
The conditional information term below measures how much the rationale changes the predictive distribution.
</MathBlock>

$$
I(Z;Y\mid X)=\mathbb{E}\left[\log \frac{p(Y\mid X,Z)}{p(Y\mid X)}\right].
$$

<Theorem title="Toy sufficiency criterion" label="thm:sufficiency">
If $Y\perp X\mid Z$, then the transcript $Z$ is sufficient for prediction in this toy model.
</Theorem>

<Proof>
The conditional independence assumption gives \(p(Y\mid X,Z)=p(Y\mid Z)\). Substituting this into the risk decomposition removes the direct dependence on \(X\).
</Proof>

<Callout type="warning" title="Interpretability caveat">
High mutual information does not imply that a transcript is faithful to the model's internal computation.
</Callout>
